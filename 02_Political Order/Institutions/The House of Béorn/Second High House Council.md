**Institution type:** Elected council and ministerial chamber
**Seat:** [[Kallindra]]
**Members:** 400 in the full constitutional model; 300 in recent practice
**Election cycle:** 12 years
**Voting threshold:** Two-thirds majority
**Affiliation:** [[House of Béorn]]

## Overview

The Second High House Council is the larger and more openly political chamber of the Falhaezrian state. It sits in [[Kallindra]] and carries much of the daily burden of law, administration, taxation, ministerial work, and civil government.

It is the chamber through which the wider realm participates in rule. It does not replace the authority of [[House of Béorn]] or the [[High King]], but it gives the state breadth, motion, and administrative reach.

## Function

The [[Second High House Council|Second House]] drafts laws, oversees ministries, directs taxation, and manages much of the day-to-day government of the Isles.

Its work includes:

- civil administration
- taxation
- war supply
- port oversight
- roads
- education
- treasury
- internal order
- ministerial coordination

From its members are drawn the ministers of the realm. Above them stands the [[First Minister]], who coordinates the ministerial order and acts as the main civil counterpart to the wartime authority of the crown.

## Membership

Members of the [[Second High House Council|Second House]] are chosen by free election. Voting is tied to landholding or recognized employment within the [[Falhaezrian Isles]].

The electorate is drawn from across the major island regions. It is not limited to [[Sentra]] or [[Kallindra]].

In the full constitutional model, the [[Second High House Council|Second House]] has 400 members, with 100 seats assigned to each major quadrant of the Isles.

In recent history, the House has functioned at 300 members because of western secession and the loss or disruption of full western representation. That absence is politically loaded. Some treat it as a temporary wound in the realm. Others see it as proof that the old unity is already broken.

## Elections

Elections are held once every 12 years.

This long cycle makes the [[Second High House Council|Second House]] more stable than a short-term assembly, but still more responsive than the life-held [[First High House Council]].

## Political Character

The [[Second High House Council|Second House]] is broad, elected, ministerial, and active. It is the moving part of the constitution. It creates law, staffs government, manages ministries, and channels regional interests into the state.

It is also where resentment becomes formal politics. Port restrictions, war taxes, military service, western absence, Kallindran strain, and regional inequality all eventually reach the [[Second High House Council|Second House]].

## Relation to the [[High King]]

The [[Second High House Council|Second House]] does not stand above the [[High King]], especially in wartime. The crown remains the supreme military authority, and emergency powers can overshadow ordinary civil government.

Still, the [[Second High House Council|Second House]] matters because it controls administration, money, ministries, and formal confirmation. A named heir must be approved by the [[First High House Council]] and then confirmed by the [[Second High House Council|Second House]] before succession becomes fully lawful.

## Relation to the [[First High House Council|First House]]

The [[Second High House Council|Second House]] drafts laws. The [[First High House Council]] approves, rejects, or returns them.

The [[Second High House Council|Second House]] gives motion. The [[First High House Council|First House]] gives restraint.

This creates constant friction. Ministers may see the [[First High House Council|First House]] as slow, old, and obstructionist. [[First High House Council|First House]] members may see the [[Second High House Council|Second House]] as ambitious, unstable, and too exposed to regional agitation.

## The [[First Minister]]

The [[First Minister]] is drawn from the [[Second High House Council|Second House]] and coordinates the ministerial order. The office manages the legislative program, negotiates with the [[First High House Council|First House]], and represents the elected ministerial government in dealings with the crown.

In peacetime, the [[First Minister]] can become one of the most powerful figures in the Isles. In wartime, the office remains important but is often overshadowed by the [[High King]]’s emergency authority.

## Strengths

- Gives the wider realm a voice in central rule.
- Produces ministers and administrative leadership.
- Makes taxation and law more than royal decree.
- Connects [[Kallindra]] and the island regions to the central state.
- Allows non-dynastic political talent to rise.

## Weaknesses

- Vulnerable to factional blocs and regional resentment.
- The missing western seats undermine its legitimacy.
- [[Kallindra]]’s dominance may make outer regions suspicious.
- A two-thirds majority can make major reforms difficult.
- In wartime, it may be reduced to managing burdens imposed by the crown.

## Key Connections

**Works with:** [[High King]], [[First High House Council]], [[First Minister]], [[Kallindra]]
**Draws from:** elected representatives across the Isles
**In tension with:** crown emergency powers, [[First High House Council|First House]] restraint, regional separatists
**Controls:** law drafting, ministries, taxation, civil administration
**Depends on:** elections, regional participation, Kallindran infrastructure
