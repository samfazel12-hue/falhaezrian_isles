# House of Béorn

**Founder:** [[Béorn]] / institutional order
**Seat:** [[Skystone]] and [[Sentra]]
**Founded:** after [[Unification of Sentra]]
**Bloodroyal origin:** not a normal bloodline; institutional successor to [[Béornic]] rule
**Political role:** ruling governing order of the [[Falhaezrian Isles]]

## Banner

Affront, a brown bear with emerald green eyes and flared nostrils on a black-grey field, clutching a silver sword in its maw.

## Overview

House of Béorn is the ruling governing order of the [[Falhaezrian Isles]].

It is the source of High Kingship, high law, council authority, central state continuity, wartime command, and the constitutional order that binds the Isles together.

The House is not a simple hereditary family. It is an order of office, service, and standing. Membership may come through birth, but birth alone is not enough. Admission rests on merit, loyalty, skill, character, learning, craft, achievement, or service to the realm.

## Membership

Members of the House may be born into House-linked families, admitted through service, or raised through office and achievement.

A resident of [[Sentra]] may be received into the House regardless of birth if judged worthy. Members may keep their own family name, assume a new one, or take the common surname Beornling.

| Path into the House | Notes |
|---|---|
| Birth | House-linked blood or family standing can matter, but is not sufficient by itself. |
| Military service | Command, victory, defense, loyalty, or exceptional wartime contribution. |
| Craft achievement | Armscraft, armor-smithing, magical equipment, engineering, shipbuilding, roadwork. |
| Learning and administration | Law, state service, ministry work, archives, scholarship, governance. |
| Religious or institutional service | Service to House-recognized sacred or scholarly institutions. |
| Political elevation | Recognition by councils, crown, or House offices. |
| Exceptional loyalty | Crisis service, sacrifice, or defense of the realm. |

## High Kingship

The [[High King]] rules the united Isles and remains the supreme military authority of the realm.

The crown is dynastic in prestige, but succession is not automatic. A reigning High King may name an heir, but that choice is not lawful by royal will alone. The candidate must first be approved by the [[First High House Council]] and then confirmed by the [[Second High House Council]].

## Relation of the Two Houses

| Chamber | Character | Function |
|---|---|---|
| [[Second High House Council]] | broader, elective, ministerial, politically active | Drafts law, oversees ministries, taxation, administration. |
| [[First High House Council]] | narrower, durable, insulated, high-status | Approves, rejects, returns, advises, restrains. |

The Second House gives the realm motion. The First House gives restraint.

The structure limits purely dynastic rule, but also creates tension between elected ministers, old elites, regional demands, and the crown.

## Bloodroyal Relationship

The [[Bloodroyals]] are direct descendants of [[Béorn]] and [[Rosaria]]. They make up much of the first generation of royal houses, sacred institutions, magical orders, regional seats, and later political conflicts.

House of Béorn is not identical to the Bloodroyals. The Bloodroyals are founder-descended lines. The House is the ruling order that inherited and institutionalized Béornic authority.
