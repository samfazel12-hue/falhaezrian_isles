**Founder:** [[Béorn]] / institutional order
**Seat:** [[Skystone]] and [[Sentra]]
**Founded:** after [[Unification of Sentra]]
**Political role:** ruling governing order of the [[Falhaezrian Isles]]

## Banner

Affront, a brown bear with emerald green eyes and flared nostrils on a black-grey field, clutching a silver sword in its maw.

## Overview

The House of Béorn is the ruling order behind the [[High King]], the councils, and the central law of the [[Falhaezrian Isles]].

It is not a simple hereditary family. It grew out of Béornic rule, Bloodroyal legitimacy, military necessity, and postwar institution-building. Birth matters, but birth alone is not enough. The House claims the right to receive people through service, loyalty, office, craft, learning, military achievement, and proven usefulness to the realm.

The House is strongest in [[Sentra]], [[Kazaros]], [[Kallindra]], and the eastern shield system. It rules the wider Isles through a mix of crown command, council law, port control, military roads, old oaths, local compromise, and wartime pressure.

## Membership

Members may be born into House-linked families, raised through office, or received after exceptional service.

Common paths into the House include military command, defense of the realm, armscraft, engineering, law, administration, scholarship, sacred service, ministry work, and crisis loyalty. A received member may keep an old family name, take a new House-linked name, or use the surname [[Beornling]].

Direct Bloodroyal descendants may keep older house names. Some members are known more by office than by family.

## High Kingship

The [[High King]] is the supreme royal and wartime authority of the Isles. The seat of the High King is [[Skystone]].

Succession is dynastic in prestige but not automatic. A reigning High King may name an heir, but the candidate must be approved by the [[First High House Council]] and confirmed by the [[Second High House Council]]. In stable reigns this may look ceremonial. In dangerous reigns it becomes the fault line of the state.

War expands royal power. The present war has tilted the constitution toward the crown, port closure, requisition, military inspection, and emergency command.

## Councils

The [[First High House Council]] is the narrow, insulated, high-status chamber. It approves, restrains, advises, and preserves continuity.

The [[Second High House Council]] is broader, elective, ministerial, and politically active. It handles law, taxation, ministries, administration, and the public machinery of government.

The Second House gives the realm motion. The First House gives restraint. The crown gives command. The three do not always want the same thing.

## Seats and Reach

The House's core places are [[Skystone]], [[Sentra]], [[Kazaros]], [[Kallindra]], the [[Béornic]] heartland, [[Greenport]], [[Redcliff]], and the [[Sentran Shields]].

Its civil reach runs through House courts, ministries, port authorities, taxation, road law, tunnel secrecy, education, archives, and wartime supply.

## Allied and Dependent Powers

The House depends on powers it cannot simply absorb:

- [[House Wilgenfal]], [[House Blackarrow]], [[House Sevensmiths]], [[House Mossdeep]], [[House Saltbrandt]], [[House Godbrandt]], [[House Meritia]], [[House Redbrandt-Redrock]], [[House Aldermark]], [[House Santeber]], and [[House Lethoven]]
- Rhodokran ruling houses in [[Rhodokra]]
- Ignahuatl authorities in [[Culverno]]
- surface dwarves of [[Ulum]]
- Kallindran guilds, port authorities, ministries, and naval offices

Some are loyal. Some are useful. Some are resentful. Most are more than one of those.

## Bloodroyal Relationship

The [[Bloodroyals]] are direct descendants of [[Béorn]] and [[Rosaria]]. They created many of the founder houses, sacred institutions, regional seats, magical inheritances, and later political crises.

The House of Béorn is not identical to the Bloodroyals. The Bloodroyals are founder-descended lines. The House is the state order that inherited, contained, and outlived them.

Key Bloodroyal legacies include [[Brandt Beornson]], [[Gorman of Meritia]], [[Anders of Deepwoodrest]], [[Augus the Learned]], [[Alfrid the Forgeborn]], [[Eldric of House Aldermark]], [[Berkar Blackarrow]], [[Ella of House Santeber]], [[Donella]], and [[Frega the Defiant]].

The [[Bloodroyal War]] is the reason the House is suspicious of eldest-line absolutism.

## History

The House begins with [[Unification of Sentra]] and the authority of [[Béorn]]. The sending-out of the Bloodroyals created houses, seats, institutions, and rival claims. The [[Bloodroyal War]] broke the idea that founder seniority could rule the realm by itself.

[[Samwise Greensteel Wilgenfal]] changed the High Kingship by tying kingship to victory, recognition, and institutional consent. Later wars against the [[Continental Empire]] widened the House's authority beyond Sentra.

The [[Western Betrayal]], [[Dragon War]], [[Port War]], and [[Present War]] all strengthened the House's habit of command while leaving grievances behind.

## Claims and Grievances

The House claims supreme authority through High Kingship, council law, defense of the Isles, port control, taxation, roads, passes, succession approval, and admission by merit.

The grievances against it are just as real:

- western retaliation and broken representation
- Kallindran port restriction and Greenport privilege
- Rhodokran legal obstruction
- Culverno's serpentglass and religious autonomy
- Redcliff as a House enclave on [[Ulum]]
- Godbrandt control over [[Meritia]]
- emergency powers becoming normal government
- tunnel secrecy and boundary crossing
- grain levies and wartime extraction
