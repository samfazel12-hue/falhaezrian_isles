## Overview

Rhodokra is the oldest continuously inhabited settlement on [[Sentra]] and likely in the [[Falhaezrian Isles]]. It began as a pre-Béornic lake-town and became a major inland trade hub.

The city is ruled by a half-elven mercantile elite whose influence rests on trade, inheritance, debt, contracts, and long-lived family networks.

## Geography and Access

Rhodokra stands at a natural lake and river junction near the [[Great Pearl]]. Goods move through the city by lake, river, road, ferry, and market square.

Its routes connect the [[Sentran Breadbasket]], northern timber districts, the [[Kazaros]], [[Bastel]], [[Bearhaven]], and [[Kallindra]].

## Appearance

Rhodokra grew in layers around the old lake settlement. Older streets, temple foundations, merchant halls, warehouses, and later stone repairs sit against each other.

The city is known for patchwork stone from many quarries: mustard, blue, red, green, white, grey, and black. Some districts still preserve timber foundations and older lake-town structures beneath later civic buildings.

## Economy and Function

Rhodokra channels timber from the north, metals from the [[Kazaros]], and grain from the plains. It is a redistribution city rather than a local market town.

Its old squares and specialist markets divide trade by craft, material, and privilege.

## Politics and Power

Rhodokra is formally under the wider House order but keeps substantial local autonomy.

The half-elven ruling houses control trade contracts, warehouses, market rights, civic offices, and debt networks. The crown can pressure the city, but replacing its local machinery would disrupt the basin economy.

## History

Rhodokra predates [[Béorn]]. Its earliest form was a small lake settlement with wooden huts, landing places, and a temple.

The city expanded through trade rather than conquest. Its old families gained power through lake traffic, contracts, grain storage, timber movement, and long-term lending.
