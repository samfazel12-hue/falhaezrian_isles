## Overview

Kallindra is one of the main political, commercial, and logistical centres of the [[Falhaezrian Isles]]. It is the seat of the [[Second High House Council]] and the largest wartime trade funnel in the eastern realm.

The city has five deep-water ports, inland water access, major gates, civic districts, merchant guilds, criminal networks, military inspectors, and House-administered port authority.

Kallindra is close to [[House of Béorn]], but not simple House territory. Its power is divided between council government, crown agents, guilds, ports, smugglers, civic factions, and wartime military offices.

## Geography and Access

Kallindra sits on the eastern maritime system around [[Sentra]] and the [[Sentran Shields]]. Its harbours connect inland goods, eastern shield routes, southern waters, and protected trade corridors.

The city also receives goods from the [[Great Pearl]] basin through lake, river, and road systems. Grain, timber, metalwork, military supplies, passengers, refugees, officials, soldiers, and contraband all pass through Kallindra.

Access is controlled through ports, gates, inspections, customs houses, pilot routes, harbour chains, river crossings, and military patrols.

## Political Status

Kallindra is both a city and a governing machine.

It contains the [[Second High House Council]], which makes it central to law, taxation, ministries, elections, and civil administration. At the same time, the city has its own port districts, guild authorities, dock communities, criminal interests, and civic memory.

The present war has increased crown and military pressure. Ports are restricted, ships are inspected, trade is narrowed into protected channels, and emergency rule has become normal in parts of the city.

## People and Culture

Kallindra is urban, maritime, bureaucratic, and factional.

Major populations include:

- merchants
- guild officials
- dockworkers
- sailors
- soldiers
- port inspectors
- clerks
- ministers
- smugglers
- shipwrights
- prostitutes and shore-leave workers
- civic families
- refugees
- displaced [[Meritians]]
- House agents
- foreign or semi-foreign traders under watch

The city depends on labour as much as law. A closed dock, delayed inspection, missing pilot, forged cargo seal, or guild strike can affect the whole eastern supply system.

## History

Kallindra rose through trade, ports, and administration. Its oldest port, [[Oldenhaven]], became a merchant-guild institution. [[Newhaven]] grew as its rival and developed stronger shadow logistics.

The [[Great Kallindran Unrest]] left lasting marks, especially around the [[Port of the People]], which was taken by force. The city’s present order still contains civic wounds beneath the port economy.

The present war and pirate pressure have made Kallindra more important and more strained. Trade has narrowed into protected channels, ships are inspected heavily, and port authority has moved closer to military authority.
