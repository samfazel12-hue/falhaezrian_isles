## Overview

Kallindra is one of the main political, commercial, and logistical centres of the [[Falhaezrian Isles]].

It is the seat of the [[Second High House Council]] and the largest wartime trade funnel in the eastern realm. It has five deep-water ports, inland water access, major gates, civic districts, merchant guilds, criminal networks, military inspectors, and House-administered port authority.

Kallindra is close to [[House of Béorn]], but not simple House territory. Power is divided between council government, crown agents, guilds, port authorities, smugglers, civic factions, dock labour, and wartime military offices.

## Geography and Access

Kallindra sits on the eastern maritime system around [[Sentra]] and the [[Sentran Shields]]. Its harbours connect inland goods, shield routes, southern waters, and protected trade corridors.

Goods from the [[Great Pearl Basin]] arrive through lake, river, and road systems. Grain, timber, metalwork, serpentglass, military supplies, passengers, refugees, officials, soldiers, and contraband all pass through the city.

Access is controlled through ports, gates, customs houses, harbour chains, pilot routes, river crossings, inspections, and patrols.

## Government and Power

Kallindra is both city and governing machine.

The [[Second High House Council]] makes it central to law, taxation, ministries, elections, and civil administration. The ports make it essential to war supply. The guilds and dock districts make it hard to rule cleanly.

The present war has strengthened crown and military pressure. Ports are restricted, ships inspected, trade narrowed into protected channels, and emergency rule has become normal in parts of the city.

## Districts and Fortifications

The gates and old towers matter because the city is not only a port. It is a controlled passage between sea, river, council, and inland supply.

## People and Culture

Kallindra is urban, maritime, bureaucratic, and factional.

Its people include merchants, guild officials, dockworkers, sailors, soldiers, port inspectors, clerks, ministers, smugglers, shipwrights, prostitutes and shore-leave workers, civic families, refugees, displaced [[Meritians]], House agents, and watched foreign traders.

The city depends on labour as much as law. A closed dock, delayed inspection, missing pilot, forged cargo seal, or guild strike can affect the whole eastern supply system.

## History

Kallindra rose through trade, and ports.

[[Oldenhaven]] became a merchant-guild institution. [[Newhaven]] grew as its rival and developed stronger shadow logistics. The [[Great Kallindran Unrest]] left lasting marks, especially around the [[Port of the People]], which was taken by force.

The present war and pirate pressure have made Kallindra more important and more strained.
