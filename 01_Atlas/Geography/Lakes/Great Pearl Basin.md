## Overview

The Great Pearl Basin is the inland water and river system centred on the [[Great Pearl]].

It receives water from the [[Norgon]] and [[Sargon]], connects to the [[Let]] and [[Erkzis]] through central Sentran drainage, and drains south through the [[Saronago]] toward [[Kallindra]] and the sea.

The basin is one of the main logistical systems of [[Sentra]]. Grain, timber, metals, passengers, troops, taxes, ferries, fish, river craft, lake cargo, and rumors move through it.

## Settlements

The main basin settlements are [[Rhodokra]], [[Bastel]], [[Bearhaven]], [[Meritia]], [[Ashton]], [[Hyrcsis]], and [[Lakewatch]].

Rhodokra is the old lake-town and trade hub. Bastel sits at a major river confluence. Bearhaven handles lake transfer, storage, and inspection. Meritia carries sacred and scholarly weight through [[House Meritia]], [[Augulector Library]], [[Temple of Heurikissen]], and later [[House Godbrandt]] control.

## Political Status

The basin is formally under [[House of Béorn]], but practical control is divided.

The crown needs food, transit, military supply, taxation, and inspections. Local towns control the actual machinery. [[Rhodokra]] has older urban autonomy and commercial reach. [[Bastel]] controls inland flow. [[Bearhaven]] handles lake-port transfer. [[Meritia]] remains politically sensitive because of sacred institutions, displacement, and Godbrandt control.

## Economy

The basin moves goods before they enter maritime trade.

Major flows include grain from the [[Sentran Breadbasket]], timber from northern and central forests, metals from [[Kazaros]], fish from the Great Pearl, reeds and wetland goods from the [[Bürkenbog]], records from Meritia and Rhodokra, ferry rights, toll income, and wartime military supply.

During the present war, inspection, requisition, and corruption have made the basin more valuable and more brittle.

## Sacred and Historical Layer

The basin predates Béornic unification. [[Rhodokra]] began as a lake settlement before becoming a major trade hub.

[[Meritia]] became a sacred and scholarly island through House Meritia, the Augulector Library, and the Temple of Heurikissen. Later Godbrandt control made the island a political wound as well as a religious centre.

## Hazards

The [[Bürkenbog]] hides routes, bodies, disease, drowned trees, and smuggling paths. The [[Erkzis]] floods suddenly. Lake storms delay ferries and wreck cargo. Wartime bottlenecks at Bearhaven, Rhodokra, and downstream routes create bribery and shortages.
