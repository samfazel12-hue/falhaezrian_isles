## Overview

Raventide is an island off the coast of [[Kallindra]]. It belongs to the eastern shield field and helps guard the approaches to the [[Bay of Kashti]].

Its main known site is [[Crowsnest]], a watchtower and lighthouse. That gives Raventide a clear function: warning, navigation, and coastal surveillance.

## Geography

Raventide lies close enough to [[Kallindra]] to matter directly to the city’s maritime security. It is not a remote outer island like [[Lonely Rock]]. It is part of the near defensive waters around the capital region.

The island is where [[Kallindra]]’s city-world and the open sea meet: close to trade, close to military patrols, and close to smugglers.

## People and Culture

Raventide’s people are likely tied to [[Kallindra]]’s port economy. Lighthouse keepers, pilots, watchmen, signal crews, fishers, and small harbour families would make sense.

Because the island guards a bay approach, local knowledge of tides, fog, and ship movement should matter.

## Political Status

Raventide is likely under strong Kallindran and crown oversight. Its position near the city makes it too strategically sensitive for loose local rule.

During wartime, the island is probably watched more heavily, especially if pirate activity has forced trade into narrower protected channels.

## History

Raventide is currently thin in canon. Its clearest developed function is as the island carrying [[Crowsnest]].

That is enough to justify the note, but not enough to overbuild it. Do not invent a full dynasty or ancient myth here yet unless the map gives you a stronger reason.

## Routes and Barriers

Raventide controls sightlines and safe passage toward the [[Bay of Kashti]]. Movement is by ship, pilot route, and signal chain.

Its barriers are coastal exposure, naval inspection, and wartime restrictions.

## Strategic Value

Raventide is a near-Kallindra watch island. It helps protect one of the most important maritime approaches in the eastern Isles.

Its value increases sharply during the [[Port War]] and the present age of restricted trade.
