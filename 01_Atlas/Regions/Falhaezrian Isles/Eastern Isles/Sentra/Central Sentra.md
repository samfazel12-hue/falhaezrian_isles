**Parent region:** [[Sentra]]  
**Controlled by:** [[House of Béorn]]  
**Primary terrain:** agricultural plains, inland rivers, lake basin, remnant forests  

## Overview

Central [[Sentra]] is the productive inland belt of [[Sentra]]. It is not the symbolic heart of the crown like the [[Kazaros]], nor the western cultural frontier beyond the [[Moonhills]], nor the volcanic [[Ignahuatl]] south. Its importance is quieter and more practical: grain, river transport, timber, lake traffic, distribution towns, and old forest remnants.

The region is held together by the [[Sentran Breadbasket]], the [[Great Pearl Basin]], and the river network feeding goods toward [[Kallindra]]. It is one of the places where the everyday state actually lives: farms, mills, rafts, market towns, guarded roads, old woods, local loyalties, and settlements that matter more economically than politically.

## Geography

Central [[Sentra]] is shaped by inland water. The [[Norgon]] and [[Sargon]] drain toward the [[Great Pearl]], while the [[Let]] and [[Erkzis]] connect the Moonhill and forest margins to the agricultural interior. The [[Saronago]] carries water and goods southward from the [[Great Pearl]] toward the wider trade system.

The region includes broad productive plains, rolling ground, floodplains, vineyards, small protected hills, forest edges, bogs around the [[Great Pearl]], and remnants of the ancient rainforest that once covered much of central [[Sentra]].

The main geographic anchors are:

- [[Sentran Breadbasket]]

- [[Mosswoods]]
- [[Hyrcasos]]
- [[Lakewatch]]

## People and Culture

Central [[Sentra]] is primarily a worked landscape. Its people are farmers, millers, raftmen, traders, foresters, hunters, fishers, woodworkers, minor officials, garrison families, and market-town households. The culture is practical rather than grand.

The region is [[Béornic]] in the broad political sense, but its local communities are not interchangeable. A river town, a forest-edge settlement, a lake port, and a breadbasket village have different habits and loyalties.

The people of the inner breadbasket likely see themselves as useful and underappreciated. They feed the realm, move its goods, and supply its towns, while places like [[Rhodokra]], [[Kallindra]], [[Skystone]], and [[Torhin]] take more prestige.

## Political Status

Central [[Sentra]] is under the wider authority of [[House of Béorn]], but its local power is probably divided between town councils, landholders, crown agents, house retainers, river authorities, and military commanders where forts or staging camps exist.

The region is not politically rebellious by default. Its tension is more administrative: taxes, grain levies, wartime provisioning, road tolls, river controls, garrison supply, and resentment over who profits from transit.

The crown cannot ignore Central [[Sentra]]. Armies need food. [[Kallindra]] needs goods. The ports need inland supply. A disruption here would hurt the state even if no one in the region claims royal dignity.

## Settlements

- [[Bastel]]: largest town in central [[Sentra]] and main inland distribution point.
- [[Rhodokra]]: ancient lake-town and major trade hub with a half-elven ruling elite.
- [[Bearhaven]]: port and distribution centre on the [[Great Pearl]].
- [[Ashton]]: lumber milling town in the fertile floodplains.
- [[Hyrcsis]]: agricultural settlement near the [[Erkzis]], known for plains, rolling hills, and vineyards.
- [[Lakewatch]]: isolated forest community tied to the smallest surviving fragment of the [[Patahyrcazar]].
- [[Lordsgate]]: ancient border fort and wartime staging camp.
- [[Sunny Hill]]: protected ecological hill rather than a normal settlement.

## History

Central [[Sentra]] preserves some of the older ecological memory of the island. Before the [[Dragon War]], the [[Mosswoods]], [[Hyrcasos]], [[Noltic Woods]], and the forest later shattered into the [[Broken Forest]] formed part of a greater ancient temperate rainforest known as the [[Patahyrcazar]].

The [[Dragon War]] broke that old forest unity. Central [[Sentra]] still carries the remnants: separate woodland identities, isolated forest communities, and places where magic or local custom has replaced normal ecological recovery.

The region also grew into one of the main food and transport zones of the post-unification state. Its importance is cumulative rather than dramatic. It matters because every generation continues to move grain, timber, metals, people, and taxes through it.

## Routes and Barriers

Central [[Sentra]] is easier to cross than the [[Moonhills]] or the [[Kazaros]], but movement is still shaped by water and terrain.

Important routes include:

- river movement through [[Bastel]]
- lake traffic across the [[Great Pearl]]
- goods moving from [[Rhodokra]] and [[Bearhaven]] toward [[Kallindra]]
- forest routes through the [[Mosswoods]]
- border movement near [[Lordsgate]]
- western approaches toward [[Midgate]] and the [[Moonhills]]
- eastern approaches toward the [[Béornic]] heartland and [[Kazaros]] roads

The main barriers are seasonal flooding, boggy ground near the [[Great Pearl]], forest density, and the flash-flooding danger of the [[Erkzis]].

## Strategic Value

Central [[Sentra]] feeds and supplies the realm. That is its strategic value.

It provides:

- grain and agricultural produce
- timber and milling
- river transport
- lake distribution
- inland market towns
- routes between east, west, south, and [[Kallindra]]
- provisioning for armies and ports

In wartime, Central [[Sentra]] becomes more important, not less. Grain levies, military staging, transport control, and supply protection all tighten here.

## Current Tensions

Central [[Sentra]]’s likely tensions are not grand succession disputes. They are practical pressures.

- wartime grain levies
- increased transport taxes
- military requisitioning
- resentment toward [[Kallindra]]’s control over trade
- flooding along the [[Erkzis]]
- forest isolation in [[Lakewatch]]
- smuggling through lake and river routes
- local anger when food leaves the region while prices rise at home
- crown pressure on [[Lordsgate]] during mobilization

## Notes for Use

Central [[Sentra]] should not be written like a glamorous capital region. Its strength is logistical. Make it feel lived-in, damp in places, fertile in others, full of old paths, river talk, grain barges, market resentment, and forest memory.

If the region becomes too clean or too politically passive, it gets boring. The better version is productive but strained: everyone depends on it, but few outsiders respect it.
