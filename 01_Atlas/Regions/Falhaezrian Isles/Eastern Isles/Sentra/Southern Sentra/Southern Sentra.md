**Parent region:** [[Sentra]]
**Controlled by:** [[House of Béorn]] formally; [[Ignahuatl]] authority locally
**Primary terrain:** volcanic caldera, glass forest edge, southern coast, old battlefield lands
**Political role:** Ignahuatl south / serpentglass monopoly / Dragon War memory region

## Overview

Southern Sentra is the [[Ignahuatl]] region around [[Mount Vulka]], [[Culverno]], the [[Broken Forest]], serpentglass, and the southern volcanic coast.

The region belongs to [[Sentra]] in law, but Culverno holds strong practical independence through fire religion and serpentglass craft. The crown needs southern materials and roads, but it cannot treat Vulka, the fire temples, or the glass plains as ordinary crown property.

Southern Sentra is culturally distinct from the [[Béornic]] heartland. Its sacred imagination looks downward: heat, pressure, magma, smoke, gas, caldera, underworld passages, and fire beneath the world.

## Key Places

- [[Mount Vulka]]: active volcano, Ignahuatl sacred centre, and hidden deep-route site.
- [[Culverno]]: majority-Ignahuatl city, serpentglass workshops, fire temples, and southern authority.
- [[Broken Forest]]: vitrified Dragon War wound and source of serpentglass.
- [[Broken Forest Glass Plains]]: glass scars harvested for serpentglass.
- [[Last Stand]]: battlefield settlement and Ignahuatl memory-site.
- [[Lordsgate]]: old boundary fort between Béornic central lands and the Ignahuatl south.
- [[Vulkan Gulf]]: southern sea access, fishing, smuggling, and coastal movement.

## Culverno

[[Culverno]] is the region's city, craft centre, and religious centre.

Its power rests on two things: Ignahuatl sacred authority and control of serpentglass work. The city combines access to the glass plains with the tradition needed to harvest, shape, and ritually handle the material.

Culverno is subject to Sentran authority on paper. In practice it negotiates with the crown from a position of leverage.

## Ignahuatl Fire Religion

The Ignahuatl faith is chthonic rather than solar. It is concerned with useful forge-fire, raw magma, caldera pressure, smoke, gas, sleeping danger, and older depth beneath the known world.

Priests, heat-workers, serpentglass craft lineages, shrine-guards, and battlefield custodians shape southern authority as much as crown officers do.

## Serpentglass

[[Serpentglass]] is dragonfire-vitrified soil from the [[Broken Forest Glass Plains]].

It is harder than ordinary volcanic glass, carries residual heat and dragonfire magic, and cannot be reproduced by normal furnace heat. Culverno controls the craft through proximity, inherited technique, and religious authority.

Common uses include blades, tools, ritual objects, and macuahuitl-style weapons edged with serpentglass teeth.

## Broken Forest and Last Stand

The [[Broken Forest]] is the visible wound of the [[Dragon War]] in Southern Sentra: regrown woodland broken by dark reflective glass clearings.

[[Last Stand]] marks the place where Ignahuatl retreat ended. Its original name has been replaced by the event itself. The place carries battlefield memory, pilgrimage, local trade, and custody of the old defensive line.

## Dragon War Layer

Dragon War dragons entered the surface world through [[Mount Vulka]] by blood magic and volcanic deep routes.

The main southern scar is the Broken Forest. Ignahuatl memory centres on Last Stand and the dragon [[Quauhtlan]]. Several dragons were never accounted for after the war, which keeps Vulka, serpentglass, corruption, and underworld routes politically dangerous.

## Roads and Control

The main land approach runs through [[Lordsgate]] toward Culverno. Glass plain tracks connect Culverno to the Broken Forest. Pilgrimage routes climb toward Vulka shrines. Coastal routes lead toward the [[Vulkan Gulf]].

House authority is strongest at roads, gates, inspections, and requisitions. Ignahuatl authority is strongest at Vulka, Culverno, the temples, glass harvesting, and sacred memory-sites.
