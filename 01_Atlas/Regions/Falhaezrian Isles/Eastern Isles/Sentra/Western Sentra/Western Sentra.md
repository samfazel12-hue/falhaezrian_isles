## Overview

Western Sentra is the culturally distinct [[Tènnakkannin]] region west of the [[Moonhills]].

It is separated from the [[Béornic]] heartland by geography, language, memory, and the aftermath of the [[Western Betrayal]]. The region remains formally subject to [[House of Béorn]], but its loyalty is uneven and historically burdened.

Western Sentra contains [[Lake Tenekka]], [[Tek Tenekka]], [[Tek Leniss]], [[Lennishaven]], the [[Gulf of Lennis]], the [[Sandy Marshes]], ruined southwest districts, old western roads, and the main passages through the [[Moonhills]].

## Geography

Western Sentra lies beyond the [[Moonhills]], which block easy contact with central and eastern Sentra.

The region includes lake districts, marshes, coastal approaches, ruined settlements, abandoned farmland, feral road lines, and surviving pockets of old western culture. Much of the southwest remains scarred by retaliatory campaigns after the [[First Continental Invasion]].

## Political Status

Western Sentra is legally under [[House of Béorn]], but legitimacy is weak.

The House spared [[Tek Tenekka]] during the retaliation after the [[Western Betrayal]], but allowed much of the west to burn. This created a lasting split between legality and loyalty. Some westerners view Tek Tenekka as a sacred remnant. Others view it as privileged, compromised, or spared at the price of everyone else.

The region is not a clean rebellion. It is a wounded province with local loyalties, old records, ruined claims, half-abandoned roads, and people who remember different versions of the same event.

## History

Western Sentra predates [[Béornic]] unification as a distinct [[Tènnakkannin]] cultural region.

The [[Moonhills]] preserved western isolation for centuries by limiting easy contact with the Béornic east. [[Midgate]] and [[Highpass]] became the main controlled crossings.

During the [[First Continental Invasion]], several western Sentran kings and rulers aided the [[Continental Empire]] rather than [[House of Béorn]]. Their reasons varied, but the result was treated as a collective betrayal.

The retaliatory campaigns that followed were not officially sanctioned by the House. They were carried out by forces from the Eastern Isles and beyond. The House intervened only to protect [[Tek Tenekka]], whose duke had refused the betrayal and supplied intelligence to the House.

[[Tek Leniss]] was massacred. The [[Gulf of Lennis]] ran red. Much of the west was depopulated, burned, or abandoned. The destruction permanently reduced the region and left broad stretches of the southwest scarred.
