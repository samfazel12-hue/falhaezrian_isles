**Controlled by:** [[High King]] / [[House of Béorn]]  
**Political form:** maritime kingdom, island realm, anti-Continental coalition  

## Overview

The Falhaezrian Isles are a scattered island realm held together by sea routes, royal oaths, port law, council politics, and fear of the [[Continental Empire]].

At the centre stands [[Sentra]], the old royal heartland of [[House of Béorn]]. It's capital and largest and most important city, [[Kallindra]], carries much of the realm’s administration, trade, naval organisation, and wartime inspection system. 

## Core Regions

- [[Eastern Isles]]: the strongest House-aligned region, including [[Sentra]], [[Kallindra]], [[Ulum]], and the [[Sentran Shields]].
- [[Western Sentra]]: Tènnakkannin memory, western resentment, and the unresolved representation crisis.
- [[Southern Isles]]: [[Yarrowgold]], [[Surgemark]], southern sea lanes, and naval rivalry.
- [[Western Isles]], [[Northern Isles]], and [[Outer Isles]]: wider island fields still structurally unfinished in the canon.

## Political Order

The realm is ruled through [[House of Béorn]], the [[High King]], and the two High House councils.

The [[High King]] holds supreme royal and military authority, especially in wartime. The [[First High House Council]] represents older restraint, memory, legitimacy, and high approval. The [[Second High House Council]] handles law, taxation, ministries, administration, and visible political motion.

[[Kallindra]] is indispensable because it turns royal authority into paperwork, ships, inspections, customs revenue, and enforceable port law. That also makes it dangerous. Whoever controls Kallindra’s docks, ministries, guilds, and customs offices can slow or accelerate the whole realm.

House rule is strongest in the east, practical in Rhodokra, militarised in Kazaros, negotiated in Ulum and the south, and awkward in the west.

## Geography and Resources

The Isles run on movement. Roads, tunnels, ferries, ports, lake routes, straits, and sea lanes matter more than neat borders.

Central Sentra feeds the realm. Kazaros protects the crown. Kallindra moves goods, law, ships, and money. Ulum provides metals, stone, roads, and northern depth. Southern Sentra provides serpentglass, volcanic authority, and religious weight. The shield islands protect the eastern approaches. The southern gulf remains harder to control.

## Defense

Defense is layered rather than centralised.

The [[Present War]] has made inspection normal. Port restriction, convoy control, requisition, emergency law, and suspicion of foreign contact now shape everyday life in the larger ports.

## Historical Memory

The Isles do not share one clean history.

House Béorn tells the story of unification, protection, bloodline, oath, and survival. Western memory keeps older Tènnakkannin grievance alive. Rhodokra remembers lake power and local autonomy. The south remembers fire, dragons, exile, and sacred wounds. Yarrowgold remembers betrayal and dispossession. Kallindra remembers contracts, ports, councils, and the price of keeping the realm functioning.

## Current Pressure Points

- The crown needs unity, but many regions obey because war makes disobedience dangerous.
- Kallindra is loyal, but too necessary to be harmless.
- The west remains politically bitter and underrepresented.
- Yarrowgold has enough grievance to be dangerous.
- The southern gulf is not fully secure.
- The Continental Empire gives the realm a common enemy, but also justifies emergency powers.
