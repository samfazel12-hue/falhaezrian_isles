## Overview

Deepwoodrest is the woodland seat of [[Anders of Deepwoodrest]] and later the centre of [[House Mossdeep]].

The castle is built around the grave of an old sycamore grove. The trees were turned to stone by magic, leaving hollow trunks, mounded roots, petrified chambers, and a dead grove hard enough to support a fortress. Deepwoodrest grew out of that grove in layers: stone trees below, boulders and packed walling above, red roadstone halls over that, and timber structures swallowed by the forest at the top.

Its strength comes from terrain, concealment, old woodland routes, local guides, and the difficulty of moving through the [[Mosswoods]] without permission.

Deepwoodrest also marks the point where part of the Brandt inheritance split away from [[House Brandt]]. [[Sycar of House Mossdeep]] rooted his line in the woodland seat of his uncle Anders, giving [[House Mossdeep]] its own terrain, memory, and legitimacy.

## Physical Structure

Deepwoodrest is not built on cleared ground. It is built into the remains of a magically petrified sycamore grove.

The lowest levels lie inside the hollows, roots, and mounds of the dead stone trees. Some rooms are formed from hollow trunks. Others are dug or built between petrified roots and earth-heavy rises. These lower spaces are old, uneven, damp, and difficult for strangers to read.

Above the grove-foundation are boulders and heavy rocks fitted into the dead tree mounds. Clay, plaster, straw, and packed binding material fill the gaps between stone, root, and wall. Moss grows across the joints, ledges, cracks, and old mortar.

Above that sits the red roadstone level built by [[Anders of Deepwoodrest]]. This is the most formal and deliberate part of the castle. It contains the main hall, the map-table, house chambers, and older ceremonial spaces. The roadstone gives the hall-level a different character from the earth-root chambers below and the timber structures above.

The upper works are made from stacked timber, galleries, roofs, walkways, watch platforms, and wooden chambers. The living forest grows against, over, and through these upper levels. Branches cross roofs. Roots press into seams. Moss, vines, and leaf litter cover old construction. From outside, parts of the castle look less like walls and more like a raised, inhabited piece of forest floor.

## The Petrified Sycamore Grove

The oldest part of Deepwoodrest is the stone sycamore grove beneath it.

The grove was already dead when it became the castle’s foundation. Magic turned the trees to stone, preserving trunks, hollows, root masses, and mounded ground. The petrification made the grove strong enough to build into, but kept the irregular shape of the living trees that once stood there.

The grove gives Deepwoodrest its basic form. Corridors curve around dead roots. Lower chambers open inside hollow trunks. Some walls are not built walls at all, but the sides of ancient trees turned mineral-hard. The castle’s oldest routes follow the logic of roots and trunks rather than the logic of roads and masonry.

The grove is also the reason Deepwoodrest is difficult to map. Its oldest level is not rectangular, symmetrical, or fully artificial. Parts of it are architecture. Parts are dead forest. Parts are both.

## The Map-Table

The main hall contains a great map-table of the [[Mosswoods]], made from wood, stone, metal, and inlaid markers.

It shows hills, streams, bogs, old paths, river crossings, hidden clearings, forest villages, hunting grounds, and house-controlled routes. The table is used for command, legal memory, woodland stewardship, and route control.

For [[House Mossdeep]], the table is more than decoration. It is the house’s claim made physical: Mossdeep rule depends on knowing the woods better than anyone else. Whoever controls the table controls the official memory of the forest.

Some parts may be older than the hall around it. Some markers may have been added by later lords. Some routes shown on the table may no longer exist, while other paths may have been deliberately left off.

## Strategic Function

Deepwoodrest controls woodland movement.

Its strength is not command over one road. Its strength is control over who knows the forest, who may pass, which paths are real, which crossings hold, and which routes disappear in rain, fog, flood, or deliberate misdirection.

## History

Deepwoodrest was founded by [[Anders of Deepwoodrest]], one of the Bloodroyal children of [[Béorn]] and [[Rosaria]].

Anders built the main hall level from red roadstone above the old petrified sycamore grove. Later generations expanded the keep upward with timber and outward through rough stone, clay, plaster, and moss-covered walling.

Anders left no known issue, but his seat remained one of the major woodland strongholds of Sentran memory.

After Anders’s death, [[Sycar of House Mossdeep]], eldest son of [[Brandt Beornson]], took up his uncle’s woodland legacy. Sycar made Deepwoodrest the centre of his own line and founded [[House Mossdeep]].

This gave the Mossdeep line a distinct identity from [[House Brandt]]. The house kept Bloodroyal prestige, but its authority came through forest stewardship, local knowledge, and the living domain of the [[Mosswoods]] rather than through Skystone-style dynastic seniority.

## Political Importance

Deepwoodrest makes [[House Mossdeep]] a real regional power rather than a minor cadet memory.

Its importance rests on:

- Bloodroyal foundation through [[Anders of Deepwoodrest]]
- Sycar’s break from the senior Brandt inheritance
- control of the [[Mosswoods]]
- route knowledge through central woodland terrain
- the petrified sycamore grove beneath the castle
- red roadstone hall construction from Anders’s time
- old forest communities loyal to the seat
- symbolic contrast with [[Skystone]]
- survival of Brandt-descended prestige without Brandt absolutism

## Current Status

Deepwoodrest remains active as the seat of [[House Mossdeep]] unless later canon changes this.

Its active parts include the red roadstone hall-level, the map-table chamber, household chambers, outer gates, scout lodges, timber galleries, stream crossings, stores, and dependent woodland paths.

The lower grove-level contains older chambers in petrified trunks, root-hollows, and mounds. Some are used for storage, ritual, defense, or restricted house business. Others are sealed, flooded, root-blocked, or known only through old household memory.

The upper levels require constant maintenance. The forest grows over them faster than masons and carpenters can fully resist.
