## Overview

Deepwoodrest is the woodland seat of [[Anders of Deepwoodrest]] and later the centre of [[House Mossdeep]].

The castle is built around the grave of an old sycamore grove. Magic turned the trees to stone, leaving hollow trunks, mounded roots, petrified chambers, and a dead grove strong enough to carry a fortress.

Deepwoodrest grew in layers: stone trees below, boulders and packed walling above, red roadstone halls over that, and timber structures swallowed by the forest at the top. Its strength is terrain, concealment, local guides, old woodland routes, and the difficulty of moving through the [[Mosswoods]] without permission.

It also marks the split between [[House Mossdeep]] and the senior Brandt inheritance. [[Sycar of House Mossdeep]] rooted his line in the woodland seat of his uncle Anders, giving Mossdeep its own terrain, memory, and legitimacy.

## Petrified Grove

The oldest part of Deepwoodrest is the stone sycamore grove beneath it.

The grove was dead before it became the castle's foundation. Petrification preserved trunks, hollows, roots, and mounded ground. Corridors curve around dead roots. Lower chambers open inside hollow trunks. Some walls are not built walls at all, but ancient trees turned mineral-hard.

This is why Deepwoodrest is difficult to map. Parts of it are architecture. Parts are dead forest. Parts are both.

## Map-Table

The main hall contains a great map-table of the [[Mosswoods]], made from wood, stone, metal, and inlaid markers.

It shows hills, streams, bogs, old paths, river crossings, hidden clearings, forest villages, hunting grounds, and house-controlled routes. For [[House Mossdeep]], the table is not decoration. It is the house's claim made physical.

Some routes shown on the table may no longer exist. Others may have been deliberately left off.

## History

Deepwoodrest was founded by [[Anders of Deepwoodrest]], one of the Bloodroyal children of [[Béorn]] and [[Rosaria]].

Anders built the red roadstone hall level above the petrified grove. Later generations expanded upward with timber and outward through rough stone, clay, plaster, and moss-covered walling.

After Anders's death, [[Sycar of House Mossdeep]], eldest son of [[Brandt Beornson]], took up his uncle's woodland legacy and made Deepwoodrest the centre of his own line. This gave Mossdeep Bloodroyal prestige without making it a hardline Brandt house.

## House Mossdeep

[[House Mossdeep]] is Brandt-descended but not defined by Brandt seniority politics.

Its authority comes from Deepwoodrest, the [[Mosswoods]], route knowledge, forest law, local guides, old woodland communities, and the symbolic contrast with [[Skystone]].

The connection to [[House Brandt]] remains useful and dangerous after the [[Bloodroyal War]].

## Mosswoods Relationship

Deepwoodrest and the [[Mosswoods]] are politically inseparable.

Old growth, streams, moss, wet ground, hidden clearings, woodland communities, forest law, old paths, and living overgrowth are part of the keep's power. The castle is strongest inside the woods and weaker outside them.

## Current Status

Deepwoodrest remains active as the seat of [[House Mossdeep]] unless later canon changes this.

The red roadstone hall, map-table chamber, household rooms, outer gates, scout lodges, timber galleries, stores, and dependent woodland paths remain in use. The lower grove-level contains older chambers in petrified trunks, root-hollows, and mounds. Some are used; others are sealed, flooded, root-blocked, or known only through household memory.

The upper levels require constant maintenance. The forest grows over them faster than masons and carpenters can fully resist.
