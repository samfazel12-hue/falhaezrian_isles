**Held by:** [[House of Béorn]]
**Part of:** eastern defense / gate system
**Region:** [[Eastern Sentra]]

## Overview

Greengate is the lesser of the great eastern gates, broader and less severe than [[Stonegate]] but still important.

## Physical Description

Multiple gates linked by a long curtain wall between two ridges, with a small settlement below.

## Strategic Function

Controls a broader approach where a single gate would not be enough.

## History

Earned importance during eastern defense planning and Continental threats.

## Controlled Routes

Eastern approach route.

## Garrison and Supplies

Moderate to large garrison spread across wall and gates.

## Vulnerabilities

Broader ground means more points of pressure than [[Stonegate]].

## Political Importance

Useful for showing that not all fortifications solve the same terrain problem.
