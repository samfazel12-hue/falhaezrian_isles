**Pantheon:** [[Ignahuatl]] chthonic fire pantheon
**Worshipped by:** [[Ignahuatl]] people of [[Southern Sentra]]
**Sacred sites:** [[Mount Vulka]], [[Culverno]], caldera, volcanic shrines

## Overview

The [[Ignahuatl]] faith is a chthonic fire religion looking downward into heat, pressure, transformation, destruction, creation, and the interior of the world. It is not sun worship.

## Core Beliefs and Practices

The pantheon is distributed across aspects of geological force: forge-fire, raw magma, sleeping caldera, smoke and gas, and something older beneath or behind them.

## Sacred Sites

- [[Mount Vulka]]
- [[Culverno]]
- [[Last Stand]]

## Relation to Political Power

The faith gives [[Culverno]] cultural authority and supports its independence from day-to-day crown control.

## History

Bound to [[Mount Vulka]], the [[Dragon War]], serpentglass, and the memory of the [[Ignahuatl]] final stand.

## Priesthood / Institutions

Priests, heat-workers, serpentglass craft lineages, caldera custodians.

## Rituals and Taboos

Likely taboos around careless flame, unlicensed descent, disrespect for gas/smoke, and misuse of serpentglass.

## Heresies or Rival Interpretations

Rival interpretations may disagree over whether the deeper thing below Vulka is god, ancestor, dragon-road, or danger.

## Everyday Role

Shapes craft, funerary practice, pilgrimage, warnings, and civic legitimacy in [[Culverno]].
