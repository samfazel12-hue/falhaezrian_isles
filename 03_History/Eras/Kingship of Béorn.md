## Overview

The Kingship of Béorn was the first unified rule over [[Sentra]].

It began after [[Béorn]] defeated [[Krova Kingkiller Yarrowgold]] at the [[Siege of Twixtriver]] and was proclaimed King of Sentra in [[2099 BHK]]. It ended with Béorn's disappearance in [[2064 BHK]].

The reign created the foundation later claimed by the [[Bloodroyals]], [[House of Béorn]], the [[High King]], and the councils. It was not yet the High Kingship of the whole [[Falhaezrian Isles]]. It was a Sentran kingship built from conquest, alliance, sacred marriage, personal command, and negotiated submission.

## Territory

Béorn's kingdom was Sentra: [[Kazaros]], the [[Great Pearl Basin]], [[Rhodokra]], [[Central Sentra]], [[Western Sentra]], [[Southern Sentra]], the [[Moonhills]], and perhaps the early [[Sentran Shields]].

The kingdom did not make Sentra culturally uniform. Rhodokra remained an old trade city. The Tènnakkannin west remained distinct. The Ignahuatl south retained sacred authority around [[Mount Vulka]]. Kazaros became the sacred and military core around [[Kacore]] and [[Jeor’s Hall]].

[[Ulum]] was outside the first Sentran kingship unless later canon changes that.

## Rule

Béorn's rule was personal and founder-centred.

The later councils, ministries, and House offices did not yet exist in their mature form. Rule depended on Béorn's authority, battlefield legitimacy, Rosaria's sacred status, oaths, tribute, hostages, garrisons, local settlements, and the conversion of enemies into bound allies.

The central problem was succession. Béorn could hold the settlement together because he was Béorn. The kingdom did not yet have a stable ordinary mechanism to replace him.

## Sacred Legitimacy

Béorn's kingship rested on victory and sacred recognition.

His defeat of Krova gave him military legitimacy. His union with [[Rosaria]] made the royal household sacred. [[Kacore]] and [[Jeor’s Hall]] anchored founder geography. The [[Bloodroyals]] turned one kingship into a set of powerful lineages.

Later House law treated this reign as the root of lawful authority, even when later institutions simplified what actually happened.

## Regional Settlement

Béorn's settlement was uneven by design:

- Kazaros became sacred-military core.
- The Great Pearl towns were bound through grain, ferries, and lake traffic.
- Rhodokra likely survived through trade value and local autonomy.
- Western Sentra was incorporated but not culturally absorbed.
- Southern Sentra accepted formal rule while preserving Ignahuatl authority.
- Moonhill passes and river crossings became state concerns.

This unevenness explains why later Sentra is unified without being culturally flat.

## Bloodroyal Household

During Béorn's life, the Bloodroyals were founder children, not yet the later great houses.

Known Bloodroyal legacies include [[Brandt Beornson]], [[Gorman of Meritia]], [[Anders of Deepwoodrest]], [[Augus the Learned]], [[Alfrid the Forgeborn]], [[Eldric of House Aldermark]], [[Berkar Blackarrow]], [[Ella of House Santeber]], [[Donella]], [[Frega the Defiant]], Krike, and unnamed children.

Their later sending-out created the [[Age of the Bloodroyals]].

## Disappearance

Béorn disappeared in [[2064 BHK]].

His disappearance ended the founder reign and exposed the weakness of the settlement: the realm had been unified by one extraordinary figure, but the next order had to be built by children, houses, oaths, and institutions.

## Legacy

The Kingship of Béorn gave Sentra unity, sacred monarchy, founder memory, road and pass control, early regional settlement, and the Bloodroyal problem.

Later generations used it as proof of lawful order. Critics could see the same reign as conquest, centralization, or the beginning of House overreach. Both readings can exist.
