## Overview

The Age of the Bloodroyals was the long era when the children and descendants of [[Béorn]] and [[Rosaria]] dominated [[Sentra]] and much of the wider [[Falhaezrian Isles]].

It followed Béorn's disappearance. The founder children were sent out from [[Jeor’s Hall]] beneath [[Kacore]] to create houses, seats, temples, castles, ports, schools, magical inheritances, cadet branches, and territorial claims.

The age built the realm and poisoned it. Founder descent gave legitimacy, but Bloodroyal longevity and cadet growth made too many lines too plausible. The final collapse came through the [[Bloodroyal War]].

## What Defined the Age

Founder blood mattered. Rosaria's divine nature gave the Bloodroyals unusual magical inheritance and long lives. The houses built real things: Meritia, Deepwoodrest, Sevensmiths, Arrowpoint, Rockgarden, Larhorak, sacred libraries, forge traditions, and mountain seats.

The same strength made the system unstable. Long-lived rulers, many descendants, cadet branches, sacred claims, magical inheritance, and old grants produced overlapping rights that ordinary law could not easily settle.

## Major Houses

The Bloodroyal age produced or shaped [[House Brandt]], [[House Meritia]], [[House Godbrandt]], [[House Mossdeep]], [[House Saltbrandt]], [[House Blackarrow]], [[House Sevensmiths]], [[House Aldermark]], [[House Santeber]], [[House Redbrandt-Redrock]], and the Frega branch.

Brandt seniority became the most dangerous inheritance. The line's prestige survived for centuries before hardening into the claim that eldest descent should rule all.

## Founder Geography

The age left politically charged places across Sentra:

- [[Jeor’s Hall]] and [[Kacore]]: sending-out and sacred founder geography
- [[Skystone]]: tied first to Brandt prestige, later to royal survival
- [[Meritia]], [[Augulector Library]], and [[Temple of Heurikissen]]: sacred and scholarly authority
- [[Deepwoodrest]] and [[Stone Rest]]: woodland Brandt/Mossdeep memory
- [[Sevensmiths]], [[Arrowpoint]], [[Bloodhorn]], [[Glacier’s Edge]], and [[Rockgarden]]: Kazaros power, craft, conquest, and waycastle culture
- [[Larhorak]], [[Kebris]], and [[The Moody Rock]]: shield-island and arcane legacies

## Political Character

This was not the later constitutional House order.

Power rested in founder descent, house seats, sacred custody, local military strength, magical inheritance, charters, marriage, and negotiated hierarchy. The Bloodroyals gave Sentra institutions, but they also made the realm dependent on house prestige.

The later [[House of Béorn]] inherited Bloodroyal legitimacy but changed its logic. Service, office, recognition, councils, and High Kingship came to limit founder blood after the Bloodroyal order failed.

## Collapse

The age did not end cleanly. It decayed through dynastic overgrowth.

The main pressures were Brandt seniority, too many legitimate lines, weak succession law, military autonomy, sacred competition, cadet ambition, regional divergence, and external pressure. [[Yarrowgold]] later exploited the civil war that grew from these tensions.

The [[Bloodroyal War]] was the terminal expression of the age.

## Legacy

The Age of the Bloodroyals created the old houses, sacred sites, magical inheritances, founder geography, and much of the realm's political memory.

It also created the warning that defines the later House: blood alone must never again be enough to rule.
