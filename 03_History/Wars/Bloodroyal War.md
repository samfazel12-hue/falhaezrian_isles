# Bloodroyal War

**Date:** [[112 BHK]]–[[0 AHK]]  
**Older duration note:** seventy years / four generations WIP  
**Location:** [[Sentra]] and wider [[Falhaezrian Isles]]  
**Era:** end of the [[Age of the Bloodroyals]]  
**Result:** defeat and exile of [[House Brandt]]; crowning of [[Samwise Greensteel Wilgenfal]] as [[High King]]  

## Overview

The Bloodroyal War was the great civil war that ended the [[Age of the Bloodroyals]].

It began after the death of [[Brandt Beornson]], eldest son of [[Béorn]] and [[Rosaria]]. A later claimant of the Brandt line tried to turn eldest-born descent into the ruling principle of Sentra and the wider Isles.

The war destroyed the old balance among the founder lines. It ended with the defeat and exile of [[House Brandt]], the rise of [[House Wilgenfal]], and the crowning of [[Samwise Greensteel Wilgenfal]] as [[High King]] in [[0 AHK]].

## Summary

The war was not only a succession dispute. It was a conflict over what kind of realm the Bloodroyals had created.

[[House Brandt]] tried to make eldest-line descent the foundation of rule. Its opponents resisted turning founder seniority into absolute political law. The southern kingdom of [[Yarrowgold]] exploited the crisis by launching an amphibious invasion and striking at [[Twixtriver Castle]].

The war turned at [[Battle of Honindra]], where the ancient holders of the Twixtriver were crushed between the [[High House Navy]] and the army of [[Samwise Greensteel Wilgenfal]]. After the victory, Samwise was proclaimed [[High King]] by the people and part of the Bloodroyal order. His first major act was to move north against [[House Brandt]].

## Course of the War

### Phase I: Brandt Succession Crisis

The death of [[Brandt Beornson]] left the eldest founder line without the same living authority.

A later Brandt claimant advanced the claim that the eldest-born branch had the strongest right to rule. This was not only a family claim. It threatened to reduce the other founder lines to permanent subordination.

### Phase II: Bloodroyal Fragmentation

Bloodroyal houses and cadet branches split over the claim.

Some supported Brandt. Some opposed Brandt. Others used the crisis to settle old territorial, marital, sacred, or military disputes.

The war spread through houses, roads, ports, castles, councils, and old founder seats.

### Phase III: Southern Intervention

[[Yarrowgold]] entered the war by amphibious invasion.

The southern attack struck at [[Twixtriver Castle]] and forced the internal war to become a broader defense crisis. This made a strong military solution more acceptable to houses and common people who might otherwise have rejected non-Bloodroyal rule.

### Phase IV: Honindra

At [[Battle of Honindra]], the [[High House Navy]] and [[Samwise Greensteel Wilgenfal]]’s army crushed the enemy force.

Samwise was proclaimed [[High King]] by the people and by part of the Bloodroyal order. The proclamation did not end the war, but it changed its political structure.

### Phase V: Northern Campaign

Samwise moved north against [[House Brandt]].

The war shifted from disputed succession into the destruction of Brandt power. Brandt holdings, allies, titles, and claims were broken or stripped.

### Phase VI: Postwar Settlement

[[House Brandt]] was defeated and exiled.

[[House Wilgenfal]] rose. The High Kingship was restructured around victory, recognition, and institutional consent rather than Bloodroyal seniority alone.

The later constitutional order of [[House of Béorn]], the [[First High House Council]], and the [[Second High House Council]] grew from this postwar necessity.

## Postwar Order

The postwar order rejected pure founder-line supremacy.

| New Principle | Meaning |
|---|---|
| High Kingship by recognition | A ruler must be accepted by institutions and political force, not merely descend from the eldest line. |
| Service over seniority | War service and defense of the realm can outrank inherited prestige. |
| Council restraint | The realm needs institutions capable of approving, checking, and legitimizing royal power. |
| Bloodroyal prestige limited | Founder descent matters, but cannot automatically command the Isles. |
| House unity over house seniority | The state must outlive any single founder branch. |
