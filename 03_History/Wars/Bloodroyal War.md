# Bloodroyal War

**Date:** [[112 BHK]]-[[0 AHK]]
**Older duration note:** seventy years / four generations
**Location:** [[Sentra]] and the wider [[Falhaezrian Isles]]
**Era:** end of the [[Age of the Bloodroyals]]
**Result:** defeat and exile of [[House Brandt]]; crowning of [[Samwise Greensteel Wilgenfal]] as [[High King]]

## Overview

The Bloodroyal War was the civil war that ended the [[Age of the Bloodroyals]].

It began after the death of [[Brandt Beornson]], eldest son of [[Béorn]] and [[Rosaria]]. A later Brandt-line claimant tried to turn eldest-born descent into the ruling principle of Sentra and the wider Isles. Other Bloodroyal houses resisted being made permanently subordinate to the senior line.

The war destroyed the old founder-line balance. It ended with [[House Brandt]] defeated and exiled, [[House Wilgenfal]] raised by military victory, and [[Samwise Greensteel Wilgenfal]] crowned High King in [[0 AHK]].

## Core Conflict

The war was a fight over what kind of realm the Bloodroyals had made.

[[House Brandt]] claimed senior founder descent. Its opponents rejected eldest-line rule as the law of the realm. [[Yarrowgold]] exploited the crisis with an amphibious intervention, striking into the Twixtriver and Honindra theatre. The war became succession crisis, civil war, southern invasion, and constitutional break all at once.

[[Battle of Honindra]] turned Samwise into the practical answer to the crisis. After the victory, he was proclaimed High King by the people and part of the Bloodroyal order. He then moved north against House Brandt.

## Main Forces

- [[House Brandt]]: senior founder line and central claimant faction.
- Anti-Brandt Bloodroyals: founder houses that opposed Brandt supremacy.
- [[Samwise Greensteel Wilgenfal]] and [[House Wilgenfal]]: military solution to the crisis.
- [[High House Navy]]: decisive naval force at [[Battle of Honindra]].
- [[Yarrowgold]]: southern kingdom that intervened during the war.
- Twixtriver / Honindra holders: local powers crushed in the decisive theatre.
- Merchant towns, sacred institutions, cadet branches, and neutral houses: pulled into the war by survival, supply, legitimacy, or fear.

### Brandt Succession Crisis

The death of [[Brandt Beornson]] left the senior founder line without his personal authority. A later Brandt claimant advanced eldest-born right as the basis for rule.

### Bloodroyal Fragmentation

Bloodroyal houses split. Some backed Brandt. Some resisted. Others used the crisis to settle older disputes over lands, marriages, sacred claims, roads, ports, and castles.

### Yarrowgold Intervention

[[Yarrowgold]] entered by amphibious invasion. The southern attack struck the Twixtriver theatre and widened the civil war into a defense crisis.

### Honindra

At [[Battle of Honindra]], the [[High House Navy]] and Samwise's army crushed the enemy force. Samwise emerged as the only figure able to end the war.

### Northern Campaign

Samwise moved north against [[House Brandt]]. The war shifted from disputed succession into the breaking of Brandt power.

### Postwar Settlement

House Brandt was stripped and exiled. The High Kingship was rebuilt around recognition, victory, council consent, and service rather than Bloodroyal seniority alone.

## Major Sites

- [[Honindra]]: decisive battle and political birth of Samwise's kingship.
- [[Twixtriver]]: old strategic site struck during the southern intervention.
- [[Skystone]]: supreme seat whose meaning changed after Brandt defeat.
- [[Kallindra]]: naval supply, administration, and later institutional consequence.
- Brandt holdings in [[Kazaros]] and northeastern Sentra: focus of the northern campaign.

## Consequences

The war ended the Bloodroyal age. Founder blood remained prestigious, but no longer sufficient.

[[House Brandt]] became the warning example: bloodline ambition nearly destroying the realm. [[House Wilgenfal]] became the model of earned military legitimacy. The later [[House of Béorn]] hardened into a governing order rather than a simple descent line. The councils gained weight because the war proved that inheritance alone could wreck the realm.

Brandt-descended branches survived in compromised forms: [[House Mossdeep]], [[House Godbrandt]], [[House Saltbrandt]], and [[House Redbrandt-Redrock]]. Their survival is useful, dangerous, and politically awkward.
