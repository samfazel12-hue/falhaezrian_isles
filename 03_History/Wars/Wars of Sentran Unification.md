## Overview

The Wars of Sentran Unification were the campaigns by which [[Béorn]] unified [[Sentra]].

Before the wars, Sentra was divided among petty kings, lake towns, mountain lords, western rulers, southern sacred authorities, fortified river holdings, old clans, and regional powers. Béorn did not create Sentra from nothing. He forced a divided island into one political order.

The wars ended in [[2100 BHK]] with the [[Siege of Twixtriver]], where Béorn defeated [[Krova Kingkiller Yarrowgold]]. In [[2099 BHK]], Béorn was proclaimed King of Sentra.

## Sentra Before Unification

Sentra was not a single state.

[[Kazaros]] had mountain powers, sacred sites, valley holds, and fortified routes. The [[Great Pearl Basin]] had lake towns, ferries, tolls, and old trade authorities. [[Rhodokra]] was already an ancient lake-town. [[Western Sentra]] had Tènnakkannin rulers beyond the [[Moonhills]]. [[Southern Sentra]] had Ignahuatl sacred authority around [[Mount Vulka]]. [[Twixtriver]] was a strategic river fortress with Rostmodya, Yarrawe, and Yarrowgold layers.

## Nature of the Wars

The wars were not one clean campaign. They were conquests, submissions, sieges, marriages, oath-takings, road clearances, river fights, hostage settlements, tribute bargains, and local rebellions.

Béorn used different methods in different regions. Some rulers were broken. Some were absorbed. Some were left useful enough to survive.

## Major Forces

- [[Béorn]] and his companions: unification force and later royal household.
- [[Rosaria]]: sacred legitimacy behind Béorn's rule.
- [[Krova Kingkiller Yarrowgold]]: final major rival defeated at Twixtriver.
- Yarrowgold-aligned forces: tied to [[Yarrawe]], Twixtriver, and southern divine kingship.
- Kazaros mountain lords, Rhodokran powers, Tènnakkannin western rulers, Ignahuatl authorities, Great Pearl towns, and river lords: defeated, allied, bargained with, or incorporated.

## Campaign Shape

Béorn first became more than one warlord among many. He gained followers, sacred legitimacy through Rosaria, and control of enough routes to make wider rule possible.

The northeastern highlands, central roads, Great Pearl approaches, and river crossings became the military spine of the future kingdom. Lake and river powers were drawn in because the new order needed grain, ferries, tolls, and trade.

Western and southern Sentra were incorporated without being culturally erased. The west remained Tènnakkannin. The south remained Ignahuatl. Formal sovereignty did not mean cultural replacement.

The final obstacle was Krova and the Yarrowgold-aligned power around [[Twixtriver]].

## Settlement After Victory

After Twixtriver, Béorn had to turn victory into rule.

The settlement likely involved oath renewal, hostages, tribute, road and river control, regional toleration, punishment of enemies, and incorporation of useful elites. Rhodokra, the west, and the Ignahuatl south survived as distinct regions under the new kingship.

## Outcome

The wars unified Sentra, removed Krova from Sentran politics, pushed Yarrowgold into enemy-memory, and created the [[Kingship of Béorn]].

They also left future problems intact: regional difference, Yarrowgold memory, Twixtriver's sacred-political layers, and no stable solution for succession after Béorn.
