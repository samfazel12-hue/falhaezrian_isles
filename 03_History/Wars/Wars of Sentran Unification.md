## Overview

The Wars of Sentran Unification were the campaigns by which [[Béorn]] unified [[Sentra]].

Before the wars, Sentra was divided among petty kings, lake towns, mountain lords, western rulers, southern sacred authorities, fortified river holdings, old clans, and regional powers.

Béorn did not create Sentra from nothing. He forced a divided island into one political order.

The wars ended in [[2100 BHK]] with the [[Siege of Twixtriver]], where Béorn defeated [[Krova Kingkiller Yarrowgold]]. In [[2099 BHK]], Béorn was proclaimed King of Sentra, beginning the [[Kingship of Béorn]].

## Nature of the Wars

The wars were not one continuous battlefield campaign. They were a long sequence of conquests, submissions, sieges, marriage settlements, oath-takings, road clearances, river fights, local rebellions, and regional bargains.

| Mode of unification | Notes |
|---|---|
| Siege | Used against forts, river castles, and hostile seats. |
| Battle | Used against rival kings and field armies. |
| Alliance | Local powers could keep status by submitting early. |
| Oath-taking | Personal allegiance to Béorn preceded later House law. |
| Tribute | Defeated rulers paid in grain, troops, road access, stores, or hostages. |
| Religious settlement | Southern and older sacred powers were incorporated without complete erasure. |
| Trade protection | Useful cities and market systems were preserved where possible. |
| Hostage-taking WIP | Likely used to secure defeated lineages. |
| Founder grants | Loyal companions and early followers received future seats and offices. |
| Road control | Military occupation of routes made unity practical. |

## Campaign Phases

### Phase I: Rise of Béorn

Béorn emerges in a divided Sentra.

He gathers followers, wins early battles, gains sacred legitimacy through [[Rosaria]], and becomes more than one warlord among many.

| Key issue | Notes |
|---|---|
| Base | likely northeastern Sentra / [[Kazaros]] sphere. |
| Early followers | future captains, oathmen, proto-house figures WIP. |
| Sacred legitimacy | Rosaria marks Béorn’s cause as more than conquest. |
| First enemies | local kings, hill lords, river toll-holders WIP. |

### Phase II: Mountain and Inland Consolidation

Béorn secures the core routes needed to rule.

The northeastern highlands, central roads, Great Pearl approaches, and river crossings become decisive. This phase gives the future kingdom its military spine.

| Key issue | Notes |
|---|---|
| [[Kazaros]] | Sacred-military core. |
| Roads | Needed for troops, grain, and tribute. |
| River crossings | Local lords lose independent toll power. |
| Great Pearl routes | Lake traffic becomes part of the new order. |

### Phase III: Submission of Lake and River Powers

Béorn brings the [[Great Pearl Basin]] and central river systems into his orbit.

Some towns submit by treaty. Others are forced. Cities like [[Rhodokra]] are likely preserved because destroying them would damage the trade system Béorn needs.

| Key issue | Notes |
|---|---|
| [[Rhodokra]] | Likely negotiated incorporation. |
| Grain routes | Become the food base of the kingdom. |
| Ferries and tolls | Shift from local rights to royal-supervised rights. |
| River lords | Defeated, absorbed, or made vassals. |

### Phase IV: Western and Southern Settlements

The west and south are incorporated without being culturally erased.

[[Western Sentra]] remains [[Tènnakkannin]]. [[Southern Sentra]] remains [[Ignahuatl]]. Béorn’s rule becomes formal sovereignty over distinct regions rather than simple cultural replacement.

| Region | Settlement |
|---|---|
| [[Western Sentra]] | Submission, treaty, or conquest WIP; culture survives. |
| [[Southern Sentra]] | Formal rule with sacred autonomy around [[Mount Vulka]]. |
| [[Moonhills]] | Pass control becomes essential. |
| [[Lordsgate]] WIP | Later boundary logic may begin here. |

### Phase V: Yarrowgold and Twixtriver

The final major obstacle is [[Krova Kingkiller Yarrowgold]] and the Yarrowgold-aligned power around [[Twixtriver]].

Twixtriver had earlier been held by [[Gartun Rostmodya]]. Gartun and his kin were put to the sword, and the castle was taken in the name of [[Yarrawe]], goddess over [[Yarrowgold]].

This made the castle both a military fortress and a sacred-political claim.

### Phase VI: Siege of Twixtriver

In [[2100 BHK]], Béorn defeats Krova at the [[Siege of Twixtriver]].

The siege ends the last major rival kingship and closes the wars of unification. The next year, Béorn is proclaimed King of Sentra.
