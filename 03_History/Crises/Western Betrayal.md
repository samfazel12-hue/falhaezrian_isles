**Date:** during [[First Continental Invasion]]
**Location:** [[Western Sentra]]
**Era:** [[First Continental Invasion]]

## Summary

During the [[First Continental Invasion]], several western Sentran rulers aided the [[Continental Empire]]. Retaliatory campaigns devastated the west; [[Tek Tenekka]] alone was spared by House intervention.

## Causes

Western rulers had varied reasons for siding with the Empire, but their combined effect was betrayal in eastern memory.

## Course of Events

Forces from across the [[Eastern Isles]] and beyond retaliated against western communities and rulers. [[Tek Leniss]] was massacred; the [[Gulf of Lennis]] ran red. The House intervened only to spare [[Tek Tenekka]].

## Outcome

Much of the southwest was burned, emptied, or ruined.

## Consequences

Western population fell permanently. [[Tek Tenekka]] became a preserved remnant; [[Lennishaven]] preserved folk memory of the massacre.

## Key Figures

- [[Duke of Tenekka]]

## Who Benefited

[[Tek Tenekka]], the House’s claim to distinguish loyal from traitor.

## Who Lost

[[Tek Leniss]], much of western [[Sentra]], [[Tènnakkannin]] communities.

## Disputed Interpretations

Was it justice, revenge, ethnic punishment, or political abandonment? This should remain disputed.
